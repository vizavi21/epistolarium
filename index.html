About

The aim is to build and expand a dynamic fully-accessible collection of Byzantine and humanist letters that document the connections between Byzantines and Latins in the late medieval Eastern Mediterranean. The website puts together resources scattered in a variety of books and studies that cannot be easily accessed. Each item in the collection has the format of a card with the summary of a letter and related information structured according to several tabs. The collection provides: An *exhaustive* list of letters relevant for the study of the trans-national connections across the Mediterranean. Filtering and sorting options according to the following criteria: language, type/genre of letter, author, addressee, year of production. Biographies Historical introductions Bibliographical lists Technology in use: Epistolarium was built with AngularJs 1.x and AngularMaterial .The collection of letters, the attached information, and the filtering criteria are included in a Javascript array of objects. Access to the data in the array is made through Angular directives and expressions. The filtering options depend on the properties key in the array. You can check and download the source-code on Github.
